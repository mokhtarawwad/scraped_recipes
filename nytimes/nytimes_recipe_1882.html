Pan-Roasted Zucchini With Cilantro-Mint Gremolata

Marian Burros

  • Yield 6 servings
  • Time25 minutes

Garlic, Mint, Zucchini

Ingredients

  • 6 slender zucchini, halved lengthwise
  • Coarse salt to taste
  • 3 tablespoons chopped cilantro leaves
  • 3 tablespoons chopped mint leaves
  • ½ clove garlic, finely chopped
  • Finely chopped zest of 1 lemon
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons dry bread crumbs
  • Extra virgin olive oil for drizzling, if desired
Email Grocery List

Preparation

  1. Put zucchini, cut sides up, on a plate and sprinkle lightly with salt. Set aside for 15 to 20 minutes.
  2. Put cilantro in a small bowl with mint, garlic, lemon zest, and salt and pepper to taste. Toss with fingertips to evenly disperse; set aside.
  3. Pour olive oil in a sauté pan large enough to hold zucchini in a single layer. Pat zucchini dry with paper towels and season lightly with pepper. Heat oil over medium-low. Add zucchini to pan, cut sides down, and cook until they are slightly soft but still hold their shape, as little as 5 minutes, adjusting heat if necessary to maintain a light golden color.
  4. Arrange zucchini on serving plate, browned sides up. Serve warm or at room temperature. Just before serving toss bread crumbs with cilantro-mint mixture and scatter over zucchini. Drizzle with additional extra virgin olive oil, if desired.
