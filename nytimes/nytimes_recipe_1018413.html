Election Cake

The New York Times

  • Yield 2 cakes

Marian Burros brought this cake to The Times in 1988, but election cakes are part of a rich tradition that predates the Republic. In May 1771, Ezekiel Williams submitted a bill to the Connecticut General Assembly for the cost of making the "cake for the election."

Connecticut elections were held in early spring, and representatives gathered in the capital in May for the vote count. These Hartford election cakes were made to serve out-of-town lodgers.

"Every woman had her own recipe, but election cake is a specific type," a Connecticut Historical Society researcher told The Times in 1983. "It was generally a yeast cake, to which was added spices and fruit such as raisins and citron and sometimes wine and brandy, depending on a household's resources."

Ingredients

  • ⅔ cup/159 milliliters warm water
  • 2 packages (4 1/2 teaspoons/13 grams) active dry yeast
  • 3 ⅔ cups (459 grams) and 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup (2 sticks/226 grams) unsalted butter, softened, plus more for greasing pans
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 2 cups/200 grams firmly packed light brown sugar
  • 1 cup/237 milliliters buttermilk
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 2 teaspoons cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground cloves
  • ½ teaspoon mace
  • ½ teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 ⅓ cups/221 grams raisins
  • 1 cup/109 grams chopped pecans

Preparation

  1. Pour water into a large mixing bowl and sprinkle the yeast over. Stir and let stand for 5 minutes to dissolve.
  2. Add 1 cup/125 grams of the flour and beat until well blended; the mixture will be quite stiff. Add the butter and beat until smooth. Then add the eggs, brown sugar, buttermilk, remaining 2 2/3 cups (334 grams) flour, baking soda, spices and salt and beat for 3 minutes.
  3. Toss the raisins and pecans with the remaining 1 tablespoon of flour to coat them; then add to the batter and stir to mix well.
  4. Divide batter evenly and place into two greased 8 1/2-by-4 1/2-by-2 1/2-inch (21-by-11-by-6-centimeter) loaf pans. Cover loosely with towel and let rest for 1 1/2 hours.
  5. Heat oven to 350 degrees (180 degrees Celsius). Bake the cakes for 1 hour to 1 hour 15 minutes, or until the center tests clean. Remove from oven and let cool in the pan for 5 minutes. Then turn out on rack and cool completely.
