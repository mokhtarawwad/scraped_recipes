Venison Osso Buco

Italian, Cranberry, Vegetable, Venison Shank

4 10

Ingredients

  • ¼ cup flour
  • Salt and black pepper
  • 4 pieces venison shank cut for osso buco, about 3 pounds, or 2 pounds venison shoulder for stew, in 2-inch chunks
  • 3 ounces smoked bacon, coarsely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 medium red onion, chopped
  • 1 stalk celery, chopped
  • 2 parsnips, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ teaspoon ground allspice
  • 1 cup fresh cranberries
  • Juice and grated zest of 1 navel orange
  • 1 tablespoon molasses
  • 1 cup dry red wine
  • 1 cup beef stock
  • 4 sprigs fresh thyme
Email Grocery List

Preparation

  1. Mix flour with salt and pepper, about 1/2 teaspoon each. Pat meat dry and dust with flour. Lightly brown bacon in a 4-quart casserole. Remove, leaving fat in the pot. Brown venison in fat on all sides over medium-high heat. Remove. Reduce heat to low, add oil, then onion, celery, parsnips and garlic. Cover and cook on low about 6 minutes, until vegetables are soft. Return bacon to pot and stir in allspice.
  2. Add cranberries, orange juice, molasses, wine and stock. Bring to a simmer, scraping bottom of pot. Return meat to pot, baste with sauce, top with thyme, cover and cook on low until meat is fork-tender, about 1 hour, basting a few times. Check seasonings and serve, with a sprinkling of orange zest on top and sauce all around.
