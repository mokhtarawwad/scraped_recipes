Mexican White Shrimp In Champagne Sauce

Molly O'Neill

  • Yield 4 servings
  • Time30 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • ¼ cup peeled and finely minced shallots
  • 1 clove garlic, peeled and finely minced
  • 1 cup Champagne
  • 1 sprig tarragon, plus additional chopped for garnish
  • ½ cup cream
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground white pepper to taste
  • 1 ½ pounds medium shrimp, preferably Mexican white,shelled and deveined
Email Grocery List

Preparation

  1. In a medium saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat and add the shallots and garlic. Saute, stirring, until the shallots are slightly softened, about 2 to 3 minutes. Add the Champagne and tarragon sprig and simmer gently until reduced by half, about 15 minutes. Remove the tarragon sprig and add the cream. Simmer for another 5 minutes. Season to taste with salt and white pepper. Set aside.
  2. Preheat the broiler and lay the shrimp in a roasting pan large enough to hold them in one layer. Sprinkle them with salt and brush 1/4 cup of the Champagne sauce over them with a pastry brush. Broil, turning once, until the shrimp are just cooked through, about 5 minutes.
  3. Gently reheat the remaining sauce over low heat. Divide the shrimp among 4 plates and spoon the sauce over them. Garnish with chopped tarragon and serve immediately.
