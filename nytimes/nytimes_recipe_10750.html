Pecan Soup

Kent Black

  • Yield 6 servings
  • Time20 minutes

Ingredients

  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 teaspoon vegetable oil
  • 1 large garlic clove, crushed
  • 8 ounces (2 cups) shelled pecans
  • 1 1/4-inch-thick slice of round country bread
  • 4 large plum tomatoes, quartered
  • 4 chipotle chilies in adobo, or to taste (available in most large supermarkets)
  • 7 cups milk
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 6 pecan halves, for garnish

Preparation

  1. In a large pot, melt butter in oil over medium heat. Add garlic, pecans and bread. Cook about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally and turning garlic and bread once, until garlic is fragrant and bread is golden.
  2. Transfer half the pecan mixture, half the tomatoes, half the chilies and 1 1/2 cups milk to a blender. Puree until smooth. Repeat with remaining pecan mixture, tomatoes, chilies and 1 1/2 cups milk.
  3. Pour mixture back into pot, along with remaining milk, salt and pepper. Bring to a boil, then simmer about 3 minutes. Serve hot, garnishing each bowl with a pecan half.
