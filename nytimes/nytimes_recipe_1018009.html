Miriam O’Connor for The New York Times

Nicole Kane, a bartender at the elegant cocktail bar at the Merchant Hotel in Belfast, Northern Ireland, devised this sophisticated rum-based cocktail. It requires a bit of work — two syrups must be made — but the results are impressive. For optimal flavor, freshly squeezed juices are essential. (Any leftover cinnamon syrup — and those chocolate bitters — can be deployed to make a delicious, surprising variation on an Old-Fashioned).

Featured in: That Belfast Spirit. 

More +

Ingredients

  • 2 oz. dark rum
  • 1 oz. freshly squeezed blood-orange juice
  • ½ oz. freshly squeezed lime juice
  • 1 tsp. molasses syrup (2:1 ratio of molasses to boiling water, cooled)
  • 1 tsp. cinnamon syrup (see recipe)
  • 1 dash chocolate bitters
  • Mint sprigs
  • Orange twist
  • Cracked cinnamon
Email Grocery List

Preparation

  1. Shake the liquid ingredients with ice, and strain into a rocks glass filled with cracked ice. Garnish with the mint sprigs, orange twist and cinnamon.
