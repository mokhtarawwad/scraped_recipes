Moong Dal

Craig Claiborne

  • Yield 6 to 8 servings

Vegetarian, Vegan, Indian, Moong Bean, Spice

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried moong beans (hulled and split yellow mung beans)
  • ½ cup corn, peanut or vegetable oil
  • ⅛ teaspoon asafetida
  • 1 small fresh hot green chili, about 2 inches long, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons finely minced garlic
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground hot red pepper or cayenne
  • 2 teaspoons ground coriander
  • 2 teaspoons salt, if desired
  • 1 cup coarsely chopped, loosely packed fresh coriander leaves
Email Grocery List

Preparation

  1. Put beans in a bowl and add water to cover to a depth of about 2 inches above the beans. Let soak overnight. Drain.
  2. Heat oil in saucepan and add asafetida. Cook about 3 seconds and add drained beans and 1 cup water.
  3. Put green chili and garlic into a mortar and crush to a paste with pestle. Add this to beans. Sprinkle with turmeric, hot pepper, ground coriander and salt, and stir to blend. Bring to boil, cover and cook 20 minutes. If necessary to prevent dryness, add 1/4 cup water.
  4. Add chopped coriander. Cover and cook 5 minutes more.
