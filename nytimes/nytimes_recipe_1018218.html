Larry Rice’s Louisville bar, the Silver Dollar, is all about whiskey. So its version of the Moscow Mule is bourbon based. Nothing complicated here, just good Kentucky whiskey subbed for the usual vodka. Ginger syrup is used instead of ginger beer, pushing the drink very close to whiskey sour territory, albeit one served under a mound of crushed ice.

Bourbon, Ginger Syrup

5 19

Ingredients

  • 2 ounces bourbon, preferably Wild Turkey 101
  • 1 ounce ginger syrup (see note)
  • ½ ounce lime juice

Preparation

  1. Fill a mug, preferably copper, three-quarters full with crushed ice. In a cocktail shaker three-quarters filled with ice cubes, shake all ingredients for 15 seconds and strain into the mug. Top with more crushed ice.

Tip

  • To make ginger syrup, combine 1 cup of simple syrup (made of 1 cup demerara sugar combined with 1 cup water) with 2 ounces freshly juiced ginger.
