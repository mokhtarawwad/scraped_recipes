Sauteed Fruit And Red Berry Sorbet

Bryan Miller

  • Yield 4 servings

Apple, Raspberry, Strawberry

Ingredients

For the syrup:

  • 4 cups strawberries, washed, stems removed and sliced into eighths
  • 2 pints raspberries, washed
  • 1 ¼ cups sugar

For the sorbet:

  • 2 cups reserved berries
  • 2 cups plain yogurt, drained
  • 1 cup creme fraiche or heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • ¼ cup sugar
  • 3 small pears, skin on, cores removed and quartered
  • 1 large Granny Smith apple, skin on, cored and cut into 8 wedges
  • 2 branches fresh rhubarb, trimmed and each cut into 4 pieces
  • 4 clusters of about 6 grapes each, preferably red grapes
  • 1 cup reserved berry juice
  • Juice of 1/2 lemon

Preparation

  1. In a large bowl, combine the strawberries, raspberries and 1 1/4 cups sugar. Toss well.
  2. Heat water in a pot or in the bottom of a double boiler (about 2 inches). Place the fruit bowl over the simmering water, cover and cook for an hour.
  3. Strain the berries through a fine sieve into a bowl until all the juice is removed. Set aside the juice. Reserve the cooked berries.
  4. Combine all sorbet ingredients in a bowl, and blend well. Place in a shallow tray. Freeze for two hours.
  5. Place butter and 1/4 cup sugar in a large pan with pear, apple and rhubarb pieces in one layer. Over medium-high heat, cook all fruit until tender, about 5 minutes. Add grapes.
  6. Add reserved berry juice and lemon juice. Cover, and reduce the juice by half.
  7. Place some of the cooked fruit mixture on a plate next to a scoop of the frozen sorbet. Spoon juice from pan, and pour over everything.
